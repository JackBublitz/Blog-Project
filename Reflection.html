Blog 1 - Website Emulation

23 / 07 / 20

This first task was intended for us to showcase our knowledge of HTML and CSS, seeing as I was entering the class with no knowledge of either, other than there basic premise, I didn’t really come up with anything remotely resembling any website. However after some time spent coding and some of the miscellaneous tutorials, I revisited this task. I wanted to recreate the nav from Bandcamp, a relatively simple nav. A nav is something found on the majority of websites that are more complex than one or two pages so learning how to structure and style one could prove extremely beneficial. It uses CSS grids and includes everything from an interactive search bar with place holder text as well as buttons with hover states accurate to the official site. This was a good challenge and was the most complex thing I have made in HTML/CSS up until this point. My classmate Jamie Gambles assisted with some of the more complex elements providing a more detailed breakdown and tutorial on how they work. This task has given me a lot more confidence in HTML with familiarisation with grids being the biggest gain as formatting was something I struggled with in my previous experiences with html.

Blog 2 - FlexBox/Media Queries

13 / 08 / 20

One of the major downsides to using grids is its support, grids is still a relatively new addition to the HTML and CSS ecosystem, and before grids, flex boxes would have been the way to go. Flex boxes are useful in for making your website format shift and change depending on the size of your viewport when used in conjunction with media queries. This could help in the creation of my blog and project 3 where we are required to create flexible website that works on both mobile and desktop.

Blog 3 - Project 1

15 / 08 / 20

This was Figma based project that didn’t include any coding. Instead we had to wireframe a website for one of two briefs, I opted for the Vic brief in which I had to wireframe a replacement for the current vic course selection site. I look at the biggest contentions I have with Vic services and attempted to rectify them in my design. My biggest sticking points with the current system is the lack of unification, there a re a million different sites and pages you are thrown between, and the fact that the actual selection process is seperate from the info, you can’t see the description or availability of certain courses when selecting you have to do it all first on paper and then go through and make you selection. My wireframes strive to create a more coherent experience, allowing you to pick and drop course as you go, with the course information available at the time of selection.
